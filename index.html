Max DD / Max Drawdown shows the worst peak-to-trough drop in a player’s cumulative P&L. Example: if a player reaches +3,000, then falls to +800 before recovering, max DD is -2,200. Lower negative values mean larger downswings and higher downside risk.

Colour system: indigo is reserved for focus/selection, green/red only encode profit/loss, and muted blue-grey is used for context to reduce chart noise. ELO: estimated from pairwise within-round outcomes using K=24 and all players starting at 1000. Risk-adjusted score is average net P&L divided by standard deviation of net P&L, scaled by 100. Use it as a consistency signal, not a mathematically pure poker skill metric. Max DD is maximum drawdown: the largest cumulative P&L decline from a previous peak.
